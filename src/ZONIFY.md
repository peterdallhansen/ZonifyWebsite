**Executive summary**

• Zonify.ai sells AI-driven spatial analytics for physical venues, especially retail-oriented and high-footfall environments.

• Its public messaging emphasizes software-only deployment on top of existing cameras, unified multi-location visibility, and GDPR-compliant anonymized analytics with no facial recognition.

• For sales teams, the most persuasive angles are reduced deployment friction, richer insight than basic people counters, and measurable commercial upside across layout, leasing, staffing, marketing, and operations.

• A practical caveat: several public subpages are placeholders. Strong discovery should therefore validate packaged capabilities, deployment options, SLAs, and live customer references before late-stage procurement.

# 1\. What the company appears to sell

Across its public pages, Zonify.ai describes a platform that converts ordinary 2D video cameras into a real-time analytics layer for physical spaces. The promise is not just counting people, but understanding behavior: where visitors go, how long they stay, which zones influence other zones, how occupancy changes, and what operators should do next.

| **Layer**          | **Public label**                | **Sales meaning**                                                                                                                |
| ------------------ | ------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Capture layer      | Zonify Core                     | AI-powered anonymization, demographic profiling, behavior tracking, and benchmarking on top of existing infrastructure.          |
| Operating layer    | Analytics Hub                   | A decision-maker workspace for live monitoring, historical trends, workspaces, filters, access control, and scheduled reporting. |
| Insight layer      | AI-Powered BI                   | Natural-language querying, context around anomalies, and proactive surfacing of insights instead of dashboard-only analysis.     |
| Optimization layer | Predictive tools / digital twin | Forecasting and scenario-style analysis to help customers plan staffing, layout, traffic, and operations.                        |
| Data layer         | Integrations                    | Exports and sync into BI, data lake, spreadsheet, and API-based environments.                                                    |

## Value proposition the sales team can repeat

• Use the cameras the customer already owns, rather than introducing another hardware estate to install, maintain, and replace.

• Move from simple footfall counts to commercial, operational, and leasing intelligence.

• See activity at the level of zones, journeys, dwell-time, cross-visitation, demographics, and occupancy - not just entrances.

• Give executives, operations teams, leasing teams, and marketers one shared analytics layer.

• Stay privacy-forward through anonymized analytics and a no-facial-recognition posture.

# 2\. Target markets, buyers, and practical use cases

Public industry pages point most clearly to shopping centers, retail, supermarkets, leisure venues, airports, and public-space environments. The strongest commercial fit is where there is meaningful visitor flow, multiple zones or tenants, and a management need to justify revenue decisions with evidence.

| Buyer / stakeholder               | Pain point                                                                      | Pitch angle                                                                                             |
| --------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Shopping-center management        | Cannot prove tenant mix, anchor influence, or area value with enough precision. | Sell cross-shopping, dwell-time, zone value, and lease-supporting spatial evidence.                     |
| Retail operations                 | Layouts and staffing are based on partial or lagged signals.                    | Sell real-time traffic, zone heatmaps, queue visibility, and historical benchmarking.                   |
| Marketing / asset management      | Hard to prove campaign impact in physical space.                                | Sell before/after comparisons, demographic segmentation, and promotion impact measurement.              |
| Airport commercial teams          | Need smoother flows and stronger retail monetization in complex environments.   | Sell passenger-flow visibility, bottleneck detection, dwell opportunities, and concession optimization. |
| Security / facilities / venue ops | Need occupancy awareness and crowd alerts without heavy new infrastructure.     | Sell live occupancy, congestion detection, and alerting on top of existing cameras.                     |

## Use cases clearly supported by public messaging

• Live footfall analytics and current occupancy monitoring.

• Demographic and audience segmentation using anonymized age / gender style breakdowns.

• Queue and bottleneck monitoring for service quality and crowd comfort.

• Journey tracking and cross-visitation analysis across stores or zones.

• Heatmaps, dwell-time, and layout optimization.

• Historical comparisons, benchmarking, and retrospective trend analysis.

• Forecasting future traffic and operational demand.

• Natural-language business questioning through an AI assistant.

# 3\. Product packaging and differentiators

The public site presents Zonify.ai less as a single dashboard and more as an integrated operating system for spatial intelligence. That matters in sales because it lets the team sell a platform narrative rather than a stand-alone counting tool.

## Most important differentiators

• Software-only narrative: no new hardware required is one of the company's most repeated messages.

• Existing camera network as the sensing layer: lowers implementation friction and capex objections.

• Unified view across cameras and locations: useful for multi-site operators and portfolio-level management.

• Spatial depth: journey tracking, cross-shopping, dwell-time, and zone-level engagement move the story beyond entrance counting.

• Privacy-first framing: GDPR compliance, anonymized data, and no facial recognition reduce resistance in Europe and other privacy-sensitive environments.

• Decision support stack: filters, workspaces, scheduled reports, role-based access, and AI querying make the data more usable for non-technical buyers.

## Where it appears stronger than legacy people counters

| Legacy people-counter framing | Zonify.ai platform framing                                                          |
| ----------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Counts at entry points        | Counts plus journeys, dwell-time, cross-visitation, demographics, and zone heatmaps |
| Dedicated hardware estate     | Existing 2D cameras as input layer                                                  |
| One-purpose reporting         | Analytics hub, workspaces, filters, reports, exports, AI assistant                  |
| Operations-only story         | Operations plus leasing, marketing, and commercial strategy                         |
| Manual analysis               | Natural-language BI and proactive insight narrative                                 |

# 4\. Deployment, integration, privacy, and procurement signals

## Deployment signals

• Public messaging strongly favors cloud-native processing and plug-and-play deployment with minimal disruption.

• A public software terms document confirms Zonify.ai distributes SaaS software and states the software is commonly available 24/7/365, with a service-credit mechanism if annual average availability falls below 95%.

• The public site also references on-premises deployment as an option, although the detailed page reviewed was still a placeholder.

## Integration signals

• Public integration references include Power BI, Delta Lake, Excel, and OpenAPI.

• The integration story is framed around legacy migration from people counters, real-time sync, and low-maintenance operations.

• This suggests a good pitch path into organizations that already have BI estates and do not want another siloed analytics tool.

# 6\. Messaging framework for outreach, discovery, and pitches

## Recommended elevator pitch

Zonify.ai helps operators of physical spaces turn their existing cameras into privacy-first revenue and operations intelligence. Instead of adding more hardware, the platform uses AI to show how visitors move, dwell, cross-shop, queue, and engage - so teams can optimize layouts, staffing, leasing, campaigns, and customer experience from one analytics hub.

| Audience           | Message to lead with                                                   | Proof / support to mention                                             |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Mall / asset owner | Prove tenant performance, anchor influence, and spatial value.         | Cross-shopping, dwell-time, heatmaps, remote onboarding, retail focus. |
| Retail chain       | Improve layout, staffing, and promotion decisions without new sensors. | Existing camera reuse, real-time analytics, segmentation, reporting.   |
| Airport commercial | See where passenger dwell creates or destroys retail value.            | Flow tracking, bottlenecks, occupancy, airport advisor on team.        |
| Operations lead    | Get live occupancy and bottleneck visibility fast.                     | Real-time maps, alerts, unified camera network.                        |
| Data / BI lead     | Fit spatial data into current BI and governance stack.                 | Power BI, Delta Lake, Excel, OpenAPI, role-based access.               |

## Discovery questions worth asking

• What camera estate already exists, and how standardized is it across sites?

• Is the main business problem leasing value, store performance, queueing, congestion, marketing measurement, or reporting speed?

• Do stakeholders need zone-level insight inside venues, or only traffic at entrances?

• How important are privacy constraints, data residency, and procurement reviews?

• Where should spatial data ultimately live: inside Zonify's hub, inside the customer BI stack, or both?

• What would count as value in the first 90 days: faster rollout, lower deployment cost, a measurable operational gain, or commercial insight that was not previously visible?

## Likely objections and responses

• "We already have people counters." -> Response: Zonify.ai's story is richer than counts; it adds zone behavior, journeys, cross-shopping, dwell-time, and BI integration.

• "We do not want surveillance concerns." -> Response: lean into anonymization, GDPR-by-design, and the stated no-facial-recognition position.

• "Deployment sounds painful." -> Response: use the existing camera narrative and the public remote-onboarding milestone.

• "Our teams will not use another dashboard." -> Response: point to exports, integrations, workspaces, scheduled reports, and natural-language querying.

• "We need enterprise readiness." -> Response: bring legal entity details, software terms, access controls, auditability, and data residency discussion early.

# 7\. Risks, gaps, and what to validate before sending proposals

• Multiple product and industry detail pages are still placeholders or marked 'Article in Progress.'

• Public site copy implies both cloud and on-premises options, but the reviewed detailed deployment pages were not complete.

• Public proof is stronger on product vision and privacy posture than on named enterprise customer references.

• Forecasting, digital twin, and some industry pages are described at a high level; practical packaging and maturity should be confirmed in demo.

• Any claims about demographics accuracy, journey continuity, or ROI should be validated against current product documentation and customer results.

## What the sales team should ideally have alongside this document

• A 5-slide narrative deck with one page each for problem, platform, use cases, proof points, and deployment/privacy.

• One short security and privacy FAQ.

• One named or anonymized customer outcome sheet.

• A demo script tailored separately for shopping centers, retailers, and airports.

• A one-page integration architecture visual.
